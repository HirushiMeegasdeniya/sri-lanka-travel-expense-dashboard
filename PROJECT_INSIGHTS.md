# Project Insights

## 1. Most Expensive Destination

Arugam Bay recorded the highest average trip cost, with an average spending of LKR 145,733.33. This suggests that trips to Arugam Bay may require a higher travel budget compared to other destinations in the dataset. The higher cost may be influenced by accommodation prices, transport requirements, activity costs, or longer trip durations.

## 2. Average Cost Per Day

Colombo had the highest average cost per day at LKR 49,181.35. This shows that even if the total trip cost is not always the highest, travelers may spend more each day in this destination. Cost-per-day analysis is useful because it gives a fairer comparison between short and long trips.

## 3. Highest Expense Category

Accommodation was the largest expense category, with total spending of LKR 5,874,250. This indicates that Accommodation is the main cost driver in the travel budget. Travelers who want to reduce overall trip expenses should focus first on managing this category.

## 4. Peak vs Off-Peak Spending

Peak-season trips had an average trip cost of LKR 124,280.56, compared with LKR 101,623.53 during off-peak periods. This shows that travel season has a clear impact on spending. Travelers can potentially reduce costs by planning trips during off-peak months when accommodation and activity prices are lower.

## 5. Solo vs Family Travel

Family travelers had the highest average trip cost at LKR 160,333.87, while solo travelers had a lower average trip cost of LKR 82,775.00. This result is expected because family trips include higher accommodation, food, transport, and activity costs. However, analyzing cost per person in future versions would make this comparison even more accurate.

## 6. Monthly Spending Trend

The highest monthly spending was recorded in 2026-03, with total spending of LKR 1,349,750. This may reflect higher travel activity, seasonal demand, or more trips recorded during that month. Monthly trend analysis helps identify periods where travelers may need to plan larger budgets.
